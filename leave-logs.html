Year / Period Entitlement

Other leave types (Duty, Lieu, Maternity, Leave to Vote, No Pay, Extension, Overstay, Quarantine) are as per policy below.

1. Annual Leave

1.1 For each full calendar year (1 Jan–31 Dec) in continuous employment, an employee is entitled to 14 days Annual Leave (with pay) from the following year.

1.2 First year of employment — paid annual leave in the following/preceding year:

If employment commencedDays
On or after 1 Jan but before 1 Apr14 days
On or after 1 Apr but before 1 Jul10 days
On or after 1 Jul but before 1 Oct07 days
On or after 1 Oct04 days

1.3 No Annual Leave during Training or Probation.

1.4 Balance annual leave with prior consent of Immediate Supervisor; approved by HOD; availed at mutually convenient times.

1.5 Apply at least 07 days in advance to Immediate Supervisor (except emergency).

2. Casual Leave

2.1 07 days per calendar year (1 Jan–31 Dec) for personal matters, ill-health, or other reasonable grounds.

2.2 Probation / first year: 1 day for each completed 2 calendar months (½ day per month for first 12 months).

2.3 From the next calendar year, full 7 days from 1 January.

2.4 Procedure: Approve in advance with Supervisor; in emergency, intimate same day (e.g. phone call).

3. Duty Leave

3.1 For representing the company in official operations, sports, or other authorised activities. Granted in advance; requires Superior and HOD approval.

4. Lieu Leave

4.1 When a statutory holiday falls on Sunday, Poya day or another statutory holiday, an additional holiday may apply (Shop & Office Employees’ Act).

4.2 Eligibility: ≥8 hours worked = 1 day lieu; 4½–8 hours = ½ day. Use within same month or next month if last day of month.

4.3 Carry-forward to next month only with Board of Directors’ prior approval; otherwise Immediate Supervisor’s approval for carry-forward.

5. Maternity Leave

5.1 No qualifying period; entitlement from joining; applies whether infant is dead or alive.

5.2 84 working days with pay (no limit on number of children).

5.3 14 days pre-confinement + 70 days post-confinement. Unused pre-confinement may be taken post-confinement (5.4).

5.5 Still-born or aborted fetus: 42 working days post-confinement.

5.6 Excludes weekends and Public/Full Moon Poya holidays.

5.7 HR to send letter (register post) with leave period on intimation of birth.

5.8 Nursing intervals for child under 1 year: 2 intervals (1 hr each if no crèche; 30 min each if crèche provided); within 9-hour day; in addition to meal/rest.

6. Leave to Vote at Elections

6.1 Paid leave of not less than 4 hours to vote (Parliamentary, Presidential, Provincial Council). Full day may be granted for distant voters as per law.

7. No Pay (Unauthorized) / Authorized No Pay Absence

7.1 Unauthorized absence: no pay for that period + disciplinary action (warning, vacated post, suspension, termination). Inform by letter, telegram, email, phone or SMS before scheduled start.

7.2 Authorized No Pay at Board’s discretion.

7.3 Approved No Pay counts as service for calculation purposes.

7.4 Apply for leave immediately on return if on extended or Authorized No Pay.

8. Extension of Leave

8.1 Only in exceptional circumstances at Supervisor’s discretion. Employee must communicate in advance before expiry of granted leave with reason.

9. Overstay of Leave

9.1 Absence/overstay without intimation = Unauthorized Absence.

9.2 Company may treat as approved leave if employee has balance (at discretion).

9.3 Unauthorized leave: salary deduction (loss of pay), loss of benefits/allowances, disciplinary action.

9.4 Absent beyond original/extended leave without intimation for 3 consecutive days → deemed to have vacated post; Board may take action.

10. Quarantine Leave

10.1 For employees ordered to quarantine by PHI/medical officer/authority; in addition to other leave.

10.2 If tested positive for Covid-19 → use sick leave.

10.3 Quarantine + sick: sick leave until well; then quarantine leave again if order still in force.

10.4 Full-time employee under quarantine (not Covid-positive): full wage for quarantine period.

10.5 Only PHI/medical officer/public authority can impose quarantine. Provide quarantine certificate and PCR/RAT (if requested) after quarantine.

10.6 Other quarantine (e.g. travel): use vacation/floating holiday or leave balances.

10.7 Procedure: (a) Present medical certificate/PCR or RAT after quarantine. (b) 1st period Covid-19: 14 days company. (c) 2nd period: 7 days company + 7 days from Annual/Casual. (d) 3rd period: 14 days from Annual/Casual.
